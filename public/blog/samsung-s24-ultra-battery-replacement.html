Samsung Galaxy S24 Ultra Battery Replacement

Complete walkthrough with safety precautions and battery health tips

October 28, 2025 • Tutorials • Nexus Tech Team

The Samsung Galaxy S24 Ultra features a powerful battery system that requires careful handling during replacement. This comprehensive guide covers the complete battery replacement process, including essential safety precautions, required tools, and post-repair optimization tips to ensure optimal battery performance and longevity.

Safety Precautions

Critical Safety Information

Li-ion Battery Hazards: Samsung Galaxy S24 Ultra batteries contain volatile lithium-ion cells. Improper handling can cause fires, explosions, or severe burns. Always wear safety glasses and work in a well-ventilated area.

Pre-Repair Checklist

  • Ensure device is powered off and battery level below 20%
  • Wear insulated gloves and safety glasses
  • Work on a non-conductive, static-free surface
  • Have a fire extinguisher rated for electrical fires nearby
  • Never puncture, crush, or expose battery to extreme temperatures

Required Tools and Parts

Essential Tools
  • Plastic pry tools and opening picks
  • Phillips #00 and Pentalobe screwdrivers
  • Heat gun or iOpener for adhesive softening
  • Spudger and tweezers
  • Isopropyl alcohol (90%+) and microfiber cloths
  • Suction cup for screen removal
Genuine Samsung Parts
  • Samsung Galaxy S24 Ultra Battery (5000mAh)
  • Replacement adhesive strips
  • Screw kit (if needed)
  • Thermal paste (optional, for heat dissipation)

Step-by-Step Battery Replacement

Step 1: Device Preparation

  1. Power off the device completely
  2. Remove the SIM card tray using the eject tool
  3. Place the device screen-side down on a soft cloth
  4. Apply heat to the back glass for 2-3 minutes at 60-70°C

Step 2: Back Glass Removal

The S24 Ultra uses strong adhesive. Work slowly to avoid cracking the glass.

  1. Apply suction cup near the bottom edge
  2. Gently lift while inserting opening picks around the perimeter
  3. Use plastic pry tools to separate the adhesive
  4. Work methodically around all edges
  5. Lift the back glass carefully to avoid cable damage

Step 3: Internal Component Access

  1. Remove the 14 screws securing the midframe (save them organized)
  2. Disconnect the wireless charging coil connector
  3. Remove the metal EMI shield covering the battery connector
  4. Disconnect the battery connector using plastic pry tools
Battery Connector Warning

Never use metal tools near the battery connector. Use only plastic or nylon tools to avoid short circuits that could cause fires or explosions.

Step 4: Battery Removal

  1. Apply heat to the battery for 1-2 minutes to soften adhesive
  2. Use plastic pry tools to gently separate the battery from the frame
  3. Work slowly around the entire perimeter
  4. Lift the battery carefully - it's heavy and contains adhesive
  5. Place the old battery in a safe, fireproof container

Step 5: New Battery Installation

  1. Clean the battery compartment thoroughly with isopropyl alcohol
  2. Apply new adhesive strips to the battery (follow instructions)
  3. Remove protective liners from the new battery
  4. Position the new battery carefully in the compartment
  5. Press firmly to ensure good adhesive contact

Step 6: Reassembly

  1. Reconnect the battery connector
  2. Replace the EMI shield and secure with screws
  3. Reconnect the wireless charging coil
  4. Replace all midframe screws in the correct order
  5. Clean and replace the back glass with new adhesive

Post-Repair Testing and Optimization

Power-On and Testing

  • Power on the device and verify normal boot sequence
  • Check battery percentage and charging capability
  • Test wireless charging functionality
  • Monitor for unusual heat or swelling
  • Run battery diagnostics if available

Battery Health Optimization

Battery Care Tips

  • Initial Charging: Charge to 100% then discharge to 20% for first 3 cycles
  • Optimal Range: Keep battery between 20-80% for daily use
  • Temperature: Avoid extreme temperatures (below 0°C or above 35°C)
  • Storage: If storing device, keep battery at 50% charge
  • Calibration: Fully discharge and recharge monthly

Common Issues and Troubleshooting

Battery Not Recognized

If the device doesn't recognize the new battery, check the connector for damage or contamination. Clean with isopropyl alcohol and reseat the connection.

Rapid Battery Drain

Excessive battery drain may indicate a faulty battery or poor connection. Test with battery diagnostics and consider replacement if issues persist.

Overheating Issues

Monitor temperature during initial use. If overheating occurs, check for proper installation and consider applying new thermal paste to heat-generating components.

Why Professional Battery Replacement Matters

  • Safe handling of volatile lithium-ion technology
  • Proper installation maintaining waterproof seals
  • Optimal battery performance and longevity
  • Warranty compliance with genuine parts
  • Prevention of safety hazards from improper installation
